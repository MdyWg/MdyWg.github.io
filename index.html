The Problem:

Overfishing is one of the most significant factors that lead to a depletion in fish populations, especially when it is done at a rate faster than the fish can replenish. Not only does this cause a serious problem for fish, it also impacts other marine life as part of the collateral damage. Humans are not exempt from the impact, as some depend on fishing for their livelihoods and many others have seafood as part of their diet.
